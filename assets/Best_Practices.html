Security Best Practices

Strong Passwords

Create long, unique passwords to protect your accounts.

Use complex passwords with a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid reusing passwords across multiple accounts. Consider using a password manager to store your credentials securely.

Think Before You Click

Verify sources before opening links or attachments.

Always check the sender’s email address, hover over links to preview them, and avoid downloading unknown attachments. Attackers often disguise malicious links as trusted ones.

Lock Your Device

Keep your device secure when leaving your desk.

Always lock your device when stepping away. This prevents unauthorized access to sensitive information. Enable auto-lock features for additional security.

USB Safety

Do not connect unknown USB devices.

Unknown USB drives may contain malware that can infect your system instantly. Always use authorized and verified storage devices only.

Data Protection

Safeguard sensitive and confidential data.

Encrypt sensitive files and never share confidential information via unsecured communication channels. Always follow your organization's data protection policy.

Secure Your Devices

Keep systems updated and protected.

Regular updates ensure your device has the latest security patches. Install trusted antivirus software and avoid downloading unknown applications.
